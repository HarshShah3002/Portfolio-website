Data-Driven Optimization of Concrete Strength Prediction

  • Conducted an end-to-end predictive analytics study on a dataset of 1,030 concrete mix observations to analyze how material composition impacts compressive strength. Performed extensive exploratory data analysis (EDA) using statistical summaries, histograms, scatter plots, and correlation heatmaps to uncover relationships among key variables such as cement, water, slag, superplasticizer, and age.
  • Built and compared multiple regression models including Linear, Ridge, and Lasso Regression ,to predict concrete strength. Achieved an R¬≤ of 61.6%, identifying cement, water, superplasticizer, and age as the most significant predictors while mitigating multicollinearity through regularization techniques.
  • Designed and validated classification models including Logistic Regression, LDA, and KNN for binary strength prediction (>40 MPa), attaining a peak accuracy of 85.44% to support reliable decision-making on strength thresholds.
  • Applied cross-validation techniques (K-Fold and Validation Set Approach) and forward subset selection to refine feature selection and minimize model bias and variance.
  • Proposed an optimization framework integrating insights from Regression Tree analysis and Lean Six Sigma (DMAIC) methodology to recommend optimal material proportions, enhance process control, and improve cost efficiency in concrete production.
  • Summarized findings through interpretive visualizations and detailed statistical reports, providing actionable insights for both manufacturing optimization and predictive quality management in construction materials.
